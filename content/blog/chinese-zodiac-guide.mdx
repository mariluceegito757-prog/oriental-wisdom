## The Chinese Zodiac: More Than Animal Signs

The <GlossaryTooltip term="Sheng Xiao">Chinese zodiac</GlossaryTooltip> (<PinyinBadge pinyin="shēng xiào" />), or *Sheng Xiao*, is a 12-year cycle where each year is associated with an animal. But unlike Western sun-sign astrology, the Chinese zodiac is deeply woven into the <GlossaryTooltip term="Stem-Branch Cycle">Gan-Zhi sexagenary cycle</GlossaryTooltip> — the 60-year calendar system that also governs <GlossaryTooltip term="Ba Zi">Ba Zi</GlossaryTooltip> charts.

Your zodiac animal is determined by your birth year, but it's just the surface layer. In a full BaZi chart, each of your Four Pillars has an animal associated with it — not just the year.

## The Role of the Five Elements

Each zodiac year is also associated with one of the <GlossaryTooltip term="Wu Xing">Five Elements</GlossaryTooltip>. A Water Rat (1912, 1972) carries different energies than a Wood Rat (1924, 1984) or a Metal Rat (1960, 2020). The full 60-year cycle pairs each of the 12 animals with each of the 5 elements.

This means your "Chinese zodiac sign" is actually two layers: the animal (your Earthly Branch) and the element (your Heavenly Stem).

## Zodiac Compatibility

In Chinese astrology, certain animal signs are believed to have natural affinities or tensions. These are based on the relationships between <GlossaryTooltip term="Earthly Branch">Earthly Branches</GlossaryTooltip>:

- Tiger + Pig

### Challenging Pairs (Clashes)

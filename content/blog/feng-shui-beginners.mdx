## What Is Feng Shui?

<GlossaryTooltip term="Feng Shui">Feng Shui</GlossaryTooltip> (<PinyinBadge pinyin="fēng shuǐ" />) — literally "Wind-Water" — is the ancient Chinese art of arranging spaces to harmonize with the natural flow of <GlossaryTooltip term="Qi">Qi</GlossaryTooltip>. For over 3,000 years, Chinese architects, city planners, and families have used Feng Shui principles to design environments that support health, prosperity, and well-being.

Far from being mere superstition, many Feng Shui principles align with what modern environmental psychology tells us: our surroundings profoundly affect our mood, energy, and cognition.

## The Core Principle: Qi Flow

In Feng Shui, Qi (vital energy) moves through spaces much like wind or water. **Good Feng Shui** allows Qi to flow gently — not too fast (which scatters energy) and not too slow (which causes stagnation).

Imagine walking into a room where the furniture is arranged such that you can easily move through it, light enters naturally, and the space feels welcoming. That's good Qi flow.

## The Bagua Map

The <GlossaryTooltip term="Ba Gua">Bagua</GlossaryTooltip> (<PinyinBadge pinyin="bā guà" />) — the Eight Trigrams — is the primary diagnostic tool in Feng Shui. When overlaid on a floor plan, the Bagua divides any space into eight life areas (plus the center):

| Direction | Life Area | Element |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **North** | Career & Life Path | Water |
| **Northeast** | Knowledge & Self-Cultivation | Earth |
| **East** | Family & Health | Wood |
| **Southeast** | Wealth & Abundance | Wood |
| **South** | Fame & Reputation | Fire |
| **Southwest** | Love & Relationships | Earth |
| **West** | Creativity & Children | Metal |
| **Northwest** | Helpful People & Travel | Metal |
| **Center** | Health & Balance | Earth |

## The Five Elements in Your Space

Each area of the Bagua is associated with one of the <GlossaryTooltip term="Wu Xing">Five Elements</GlossaryTooltip>. You can strengthen an area by introducing the appropriate element through color, shape, material, or actual objects:

- **Wood** — Green and blue tones, columnar shapes, plants, wooden furniture
- **Fire** — Red and orange tones, triangular shapes, candles, lighting
- **Earth** — Yellow and brown tones, square shapes, ceramics, crystals
- **Metal** — White and metallic tones, round shapes, metal objects, stones
- **Water** — Black and dark blue tones, wavy shapes, mirrors, fountains

## Simple Feng Shui Adjustments You Can Make Today

You don't need a complete home renovation to benefit from Feng Shui. Start with these simple adjustments:

### 1. Clear the Clutter

Clutter is the single biggest blocker of Qi. Each item you keep should be either useful, beautiful, or meaningful. Everything else is stagnant energy waiting to be released.

### 2. Position Your Bed Correctly

The "commanding position" means your bed should be:
- Visible from the door (but not directly in line with it)
- Against a solid wall for support
- With space on both sides for balance

### 3. Fix What's Broken

Leaky faucets represent draining wealth. Burnt-out light bulbs dim your clarity and visibility. Broken objects hold broken energy. Repair them or let them go.

### 4. Welcome Qi at the Entrance

Your front door is the "Mouth of Qi" — the primary entry point for energy. Make sure it opens fully, is well-lit, and is free of obstructions. A healthy plant or a beautiful piece of art near the entrance invites positive energy in.

### 5. Balance the Elements

If a room feels off, check which elements dominate. A home office with too much Metal (computers, filing cabinets) may feel cold and rigid — add Wood (a plant) or Fire (a lamp) to restore balance.

## Common Feng Shui Myths

**Myth: Feng Shui requires Chinese decorations.** Feng Shui is about energy, not aesthetics. Any style can have good Feng Shui.

**Myth: You need to know your Kua number first.** While the Kua number (derived from your birth year and gender) is used in classical schools, the form school — working with the visible environment — can be practiced by anyone.

**Myth: Bad Feng Shui means bad luck.** Feng Shui is about influence, not determination. A poorly arranged space creates obstacles; a well-arranged one removes them. Your choices and actions still matter most.

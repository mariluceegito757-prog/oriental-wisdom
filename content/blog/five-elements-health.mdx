## Wu Xing and the Human Body

The <GlossaryTooltip term="Wu Xing">Five Elements</GlossaryTooltip> framework is not only a tool for astrology and philosophy — it is the foundation of Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM). For over two thousand years, Chinese physicians have understood health as the dynamic balance of Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, and Water within the body.

Each element governs specific organs, tissues, emotions, and functions. When one element is out of balance, a cascade of symptoms — both physical and emotional — tends to follow.

## The Five Element Organ Map

| Element | Yin Organ (Zang) | Yang Organ (Fu) | Tissue | Sense | Emotion |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|--------|-------|---------|
| **Wood** | Liver | Gallbladder | Tendons | Eyes | Anger / Kindness |
| **Fire** | Heart | Small Intestine | Blood vessels | Tongue | Joy / Hate |
| **Earth** | Spleen | Stomach | Muscles | Mouth | Worry / Empathy |
| **Metal** | Lungs | Large Intestine | Skin | Nose | Grief / Courage |
| **Water** | Kidneys | Bladder | Bones | Ears | Fear / Willpower |

## Reading Elemental Imbalances

### Wood Imbalance
When the Liver (Wood) is out of balance, you may experience:
- **Physical:** Headaches, muscle tension, eye strain, menstrual irregularities
- **Emotional:** Irritability, frustration, feeling stuck or repressed
- **Restoring balance:** Sour foods, green vegetables, gentle movement (tai chi, stretching), early bedtime

### Fire Imbalance
When the Heart (Fire) is out of balance, you may experience:
- **Physical:** Palpitations, insomnia, excessive sweating, anxiety
- **Emotional:** Restlessness, agitation, excessive joy or lack of joy
- **Restoring balance:** Bitter foods, cooling activities, meditation, adequate stillness

### Earth Imbalance
When the Spleen (Earth) is out of balance, you may experience:
- **Physical:** Fatigue, bloating, weak digestion, sugar cravings
- **Emotional:** Overthinking, worry, feeling ungrounded or scattered
- **Restoring balance:** Warm cooked foods, regular meal times, grounding exercise, reducing cold/raw foods

### Metal Imbalance
When the Lungs (Metal) are out of balance, you may experience:
- **Physical:** Frequent colds, respiratory issues, skin problems, constipation
- **Emotional:** Unprocessed grief, difficulty letting go, rigid thinking
- **Restoring balance:** Pungent foods (garlic, onion), deep breathing, time in nature, decluttering

### Water Imbalance
When the Kidneys (Water) are out of balance, you may experience:
- **Physical:** Low back pain, fatigue, hearing issues, premature aging
- **Emotional:** Fear, insecurity, lack of willpower, burnout
- **Restoring balance:** Salty foods in moderation, adequate hydration, rest, bone broths, conserving energy

## The Sheng and Ke Cycles in Health

The <GlossaryTooltip term="Sheng Cycle">Generating Cycle</GlossaryTooltip> (Sheng) and <GlossaryTooltip term="Ke Cycle">Controlling Cycle</GlossaryTooltip> (Ke) explain how organ systems support and regulate each other:

**Generating (Sheng):** Liver nourishes Heart → Heart warms Spleen → Spleen strengthens Lungs → Lungs hydrate Kidneys → Kidneys nourish Liver

**Controlling (Ke):** Liver regulates Spleen → Spleen manages Kidneys → Kidneys restrain Heart → Heart controls Lungs → Lungs moderate Liver

When these cycles function smoothly, the body maintains health effortlessly. Disease arises when a cycle is broken — for example, when the Liver (Wood) over-controls the Spleen (Earth), digestive problems follow stress.

## Emotions and the Elements

One of TCM's most profound insights is that **emotions are not separate from physical health**. Each element's emotion, when expressed in balance, is healthy. When suppressed or excessive, it damages the corresponding organ:

- Healthy anger (Wood) = setting boundaries. Excessive anger = liver stress.
- Healthy joy (Fire) = connection and warmth. Excessive stimulation = heart agitation.
- Healthy reflection (Earth) = learning from experience. Overthinking = digestive weakness.
- Healthy grief (Metal) = processing loss. Unresolved grief = lung depletion.
- Healthy caution (Water) = wisdom. Chronic fear = kidney exhaustion.

## Your BaZi and Your Health

Your <GlossaryTooltip term="Ba Zi">Ba Zi</GlossaryTooltip> chart reveals which elements are naturally strong or weak for you. A person with very weak Fire in their chart may be constitutionally prone to heart or circulatory issues — not as a fate, but as a tendency to be aware of and compensate for through lifestyle.

This is where the chart becomes practical: it doesn't predict illness, but it shows where you benefit most from conscious support.
